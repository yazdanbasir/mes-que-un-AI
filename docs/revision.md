# Revisión — Spaced Retrieval & Study

## What this tab is for

Reading builds comprehension. Retrieval builds memory. This tab is where phrases you saved while reading come back to you at the right time and ask you to produce — not just recognise — the language.

The distinction matters. Recognising a word when you see it is a much lower bar than recalling it unprompted or using it in a sentence. Acquisition science (and a lot of cognitive research) is clear: the act of retrieval — struggling to remember something — is what consolidates it into long-term memory. Re-reading your saved phrases doesn't do this. Being asked what they mean, and then having to use them, does.

---

## How the scheduling works

Phrases move through time buckets based on how well you recalled them:

| Stage | Shown again after |
|---|---|
| New | 1 day |
| Remembered | 3 days |
| Remembered again | 7 days |
| Remembered again | 14 days |
| Mastered | Retired from rotation |
| Forgotten | Reset to 1 day |

Each review session shows you phrases that are due — not all of them, just the ones whose interval has elapsed. This keeps sessions short and high-signal. 5–10 phrases a day is better than 50 once a week.

---

## Review flow

Each phrase gets two prompts:

**1. Recognition** — you're shown the Spanish phrase and asked what it means. Think first, then reveal. Mark it honestly: did you know it or didn't you?

**2. Production** — you're asked to write one sentence in Spanish using the phrase. It doesn't have to be about football. It just has to be natural. Groq evaluates your sentence and gives you one line of feedback — not a grade, just whether the usage was natural and why.

The production step is the one that actually matters. Recognition is cheap. Writing a sentence forces retrieval, construction, and usage in context — the same cognitive process that produces fluent output over time.

---

## Phrase categories *(planned)*

Each phrase is tagged at save time with a grammar category:

- **Reflexive verb** (se fue, se lesionó)
- **Subjunctive** (espera que gane, aunque llegue tarde)
- **Idiom / set phrase** (en propia puerta, fuera de juego)
- **Football vocabulary** (remate, despejar, fuera de banda)
- **General vocabulary**

Over time, the tab shows a simple breakdown of what you're saving most. Not because you need to study the pattern directly, but because it tells you where your reading is hitting resistance. If you're consistently saving subjunctive forms, that's where your input level is — and you can adjust which articles you read accordingly.

---

## What to expect over time

- **Week 1–2:** Most phrases come back unfamiliar. That's normal — you haven't encountered them enough times yet.
- **Week 3–4:** You start recalling phrases before the answer is revealed. The retrieval is getting faster.
- **Month 2+:** Phrases you mastered stop appearing. Your deck gets smaller, not bigger, even as you keep saving. That's the signal: vocabulary is landing.
- **Real test:** You read an article and a phrase you saved two weeks ago appears in the wild — and you understand it without looking it up. That's acquisition.
